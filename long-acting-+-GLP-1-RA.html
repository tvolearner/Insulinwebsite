This page focuses on all Long-Acting + GLP-1 RA Insulin medications and you can look up their information easily

Medication Name Duration Information
Xultophy [Pen]
Generic: Insulin degludec + liraglutide (analog) 		Up to 42 hours (degludec)
Indications: Type 2, not for Type 1
MOA: Combine MOA of Insulin degludec and MOA of GLP-1 agonist
Dosing (Adult): 10 units subcutaneously daily(Patients naive to basal insulin or a GLP-1 agonist)/ 16 units subcutaneously daily (Patients currently on basal insulin or a GLP-1 agonist)
Side Effects: pain and redness at the injection site, low blood sugar
Monitoring: Check blood sugar levels, check A1C every 3 - 6 months
Soliqua [SoloStar]
Generic: Insulin glargine + lixisenatide (analog) 		24 - 30 hours (glargine)
Indications: Type 2, not for Type 1
MOA: Combine MOA of Insulin glargine and MOA of GLP-1 agonist
Dosing (Adult): 15 units subcutaneously daily(Patients naive to basal insulin or a GLP-1 agonist)/ 30 units subcutaneously daily (Patients currently on basal insulin or a GLP-1 agonist)
Side Effects: pain and redness at the injection site, low blood sugar
Monitoring: Check blood sugar levels, check A1C every 3 - 6 months
